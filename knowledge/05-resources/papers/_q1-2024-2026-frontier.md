## 1. Foundation Models for Finance

| # | Title | Year | Authors | Code | Why it matters |
|---|-------|------|---------|------|----------------|
| F1 | **Kronos: A Foundation Model for the Language of Financial Markets** ✅ | 2025-08 | Yu Shi, Zongliang Fu, Shuo Chen, Bohan Zhao, Wei Xu, Changshui Zhang, Jian Li | https://github.com/shiyu-coder/Kronos | **First open-source TSFM for K-line/candlestick data**, pretrained on 12B+ K-lines from 45 global exchanges. +93% RankIC vs leading TSFM, +87% vs best non-pretrained baseline. 9% lower MAE on volatility, 22% improvement on synthetic K-line generation. **This is the canonical "Chronos-for-finance" paper.** |
| F2 | **Multivariate Financial Forecasting using the Chronos Time Series Foundation Models** | 2026-05 | (Chronos-2 authors + finance team) | uses Amazon Chronos-2 | Tests whether MV inputs beat UV for Magnificent-7 equities + UST rates. **MV beats UV in every case**, but mixing unrelated series (equity + rates) hurts. Methodological lesson: disciplined rolling protocols + related-series joint modeling. |
| F3 | **Foundation Time-Series AI Model for Realized Volatility Forecasting** | 2025-05 | (TimesFM team) | uses Google TimesFM | Fine-tuned TimesFM **statistically beats** classical GARCH-family on realized vol via Diebold-Mariano + Giacomini-White tests. Key finding: **zero-shot is not enough** — incremental fine-tuning on financial data is essential. Bridges econometrics ↔ deep learning. |
| F4 | **From Index to Equity: Pre-Training Transformers for Stock Return Prediction** | 2026 | (multi-asset pre-training team) | TBD | Transfers index-level pre-training to individual stock prediction. Improves sample efficiency on small-cap / illiquid names. |

## 🔁 Cross-cutting themes (2024-2026)

1. **Foundation models eat quant finance**: Kronos (F1), Chronos-finance (F2), TimesFM for vol (F3) — pretraining on massive financial corpora is the new SOTA, but **fine-tuning on target data is essential**.
2. **LLM as feature factory, not oracle**: Use LLMs to extract structured features from news/filings (L5, L8, L9), then feed classical models. Don't trade directly on LLM outputs (L4, L3 show limits).
3. **Spurious results are everywhere**: B1 is a must-read — many published ML strategies would fail its falsification audit.
4. **Crypto microstructure is its own field now**: C1-C7 (Polymarket, Hyperliquid) require **on-chain** ground truth — feed data is unreliable.
5. **Macro-aware models matter in stress**: T1 (HANET) shows pure price-based DL collapses in turbulent regimes; mixed-frequency attention with macro context is the answer.

## 📚 Verified canonical reads (still essential)

These remain the foundation — the 2024-2026 papers build **on top of**:

- **Markowitz (1952)** — efficient frontier (now: constrained MVO, BL, HRP, RL-augmented)
- **Sharpe (1964)** — CAPM (now: factor zoo → neural factor models, A5)
- **Black-Scholes (1973)** — option pricing (now: rough vol, Bergomi for VIX options, A4)
- **Engle (1982)** — ARCH/GARCH (now: regime-switching GARCH V2, HMM-t V1, fine-tuned DL V3)
- **Fama-French (1993)** — 3-factor (now: A1 shows it's portfolio-construction dependent)
- **Almgren-Chriss (2000)** — optimal execution (now: RL-based R2)
- **López de Prado (2014, 2018)** — deflated Sharpe, backtest overfitting (now: B1 falsification audit extends this line)
- **Gu-Kelly-Xiu (2020)** — neural asset pricing (now: A2 interpretable variants, A5 MLP comparison)
- **Harris (1986)** — market microstructure foundations (now: M1-M8 empirical + M2 square-root confirmation)
